About the Album

Inspired by the Japanese legend of the red thread of fate, the album explores themes of attachment, anxiety, nostalgia, and farewell.

The Beginning

The collaboration between Red Fingers and NVSCVR began during the pandemic. Captivated by the trio's melancholic and digital sound, Red Fingers reached out to them through social media. From the moment they connected, they started creating music together and haven't stopped since, forming an unstoppable creative partnership that continues to evolve.

Creative Connection

Over time, a creative and personal connection was consolidated: "We connected very quickly. We share references, visions, and above all a very intuitive way of working. We understood each other without explaining too much."

The Creation Process

The "HILO ROJO" album project was born in one of those sessions. Red Fingers proposed going to a family house in the countryside, near San Pedro, to create something different. "We locked ourselves up for a week in the middle of nowhere. The first songs of the album came out. Everything flowed naturally."

Epheme Records

Red Fingers—who has worked with Bad Bunny, Rauw Alejandro and Paloma Mami—released this album as the first title of his new label Epheme. "It's not just a label, it's a space for artists who want to break formulas and explore new aesthetics."

Follow @ephemerecs

Making Of

Behind the scenes of the album creation

"We didn't want to make just another album. We were looking for an emotional, aesthetic, sound narrative. It wasn't an experiment, it was an artistic decision: bringing together electronic, trap, R&B and pop from a sincere and alternative place."
— Red Fingers
